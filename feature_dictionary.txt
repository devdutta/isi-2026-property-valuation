==============================================================================
  FEATURE DICTIONARY  -  the 14 columns you predict house prices from
  AI Property Valuation Assistant  -  Module 2 (M2) of the project
==============================================================================

WHAT IT IS
  The Ames dataset has 80 columns. You do NOT use all of them. You start with
  this hand-picked subset of 14 high-signal features (13 numeric + 1 text).
  In M2 you select exactly these columns into X (the inputs); SalePrice is y
  (the thing you predict).

  >> The column names have SPACES and exact capitalisation (e.g. "Gr Liv Area",
     "Overall Qual"). Copy them EXACTLY or pandas raises KeyError.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  THE 13 NUMERIC FEATURES        (corr = correlation with SalePrice, -1..+1;
                                  higher = moves more tightly with price)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  COLUMN              corr   WHAT IT MEANS (plain English)
  ----------------    ----   --------------------------------------------------
  Overall Qual        +.81   Overall material + finish quality, rated 1-10.
                             THE strongest driver - quality beats size.
  Gr Liv Area         +.72   Above-ground living area, in square feet.
                             The main "how big is it" number.
  Total Bsmt SF       +.66   Total basement area, square feet. (1 blank row.)
  Garage Cars         +.65   How many cars the garage holds (0-4). (1 blank.)
  Garage Area         +.65   Garage size, square feet. (1 blank row.)
  1st Flr SF          +.64   First-floor area, square feet.
  Year Built          +.57   Year the house was originally built.
  Full Bath           +.54   Number of full bathrooms above ground.
  Year Remod/Add      +.54   Year of last remodel (= Year Built if never
                             remodelled).
  TotRms AbvGrd       +.50   Total rooms above ground (does NOT count
                             bathrooms).
  Fireplaces          +.47   Number of fireplaces.
  Lot Area            +.27   Total lot (land) size, square feet. Weak on its
                             own - big lots aren't always pricey.
  Bedroom AbvGr       +.14   Bedrooms above ground (NOT basement bedrooms).
                             Surprisingly weak - more bedrooms != more money
                             once size + quality are accounted for. Good
                             talking point for your report.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  THE 1 CATEGORICAL (TEXT) FEATURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Neighborhood               Which of Ames' 28 neighbourhoods the house is in
                             (e.g. NAmes, CollgCr, OldTown). It is TEXT, so M4
                             turns it into 0/1 columns with one-hot encoding -
                             a model cannot do maths on the word "NAmes".

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  THE TARGET (what you predict)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SalePrice                  The actual sale price in US dollars. This is y.
                             It is NEVER part of X - that would be cheating
                             (the model would just read off the answer).

3 RULES THAT KEEP THIS SANE
  1. copy column names EXACTLY      -> spaces + capitals matter ("Gr Liv Area")
  2. SalePrice is y, never in X     -> putting the answer in the inputs = leak
  3. split BEFORE you preprocess     -> the test set must stay unseen (see M4)

WHY THIS SUBSET (own this in your report)
  These 14 are picked for high signal + being easy to explain to a
  non-technical reader. They are a STARTING POINT, not a locked answer: once
  the pipeline runs end-to-end, you may add features from the full 80-column
  file and measure whether your R-squared improves. Document what you added
  and whether it helped - that experiment earns marks.

  Correlations above are computed on the 2,925 rows AFTER dropping De Cock's
  5 outlier sales (Gr Liv Area > 4000). They are a guide to single-feature
  signal, NOT proof of importance - the Random Forest (M7) re-ranks them once
  features interact (e.g. Garage Cars and Garage Area partly overlap).
